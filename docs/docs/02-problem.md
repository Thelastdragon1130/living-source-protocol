# Problem

Modern systems suffer from a fundamental structural flaw: authority inversion.

In many environments, platforms, institutions, and systems behave as though they are the source of authority rather than utilities that execute instructions.

This creates several problems:

## Authority Inversion
Systems begin to define users instead of serving them.

## Fragmented Identity
Individuals are split across multiple platforms with no unified origin of authority.

## Record Distortion
Intent is often lost or altered as it moves through administrative layers.

## Utility Capture
Tools that were meant to assist become gatekeepers of access and control.

## Governance Without Source Integrity
Rules are enforced without properly recognizing the initiating party.

---

The result is misalignment between human intent and system execution.

The Living Source Protocol exists to correct this structure.
